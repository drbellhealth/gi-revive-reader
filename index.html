The thing to understand is what it is for: it is a powder you mix in water that helps the lining of your small intestine rebuild itself. That lining is a single layer of cells standing between your food and the rest of you, and when it gets worn down it leaks, which is the idea behind the term 'leaky gut.'

What is GI Revive Powder?

GI Revive is one scoop that does several jobs at once. The star ingredient is L-glutamine, which is essentially food for the cells lining your gut, so it fuels the cells that do the repairing. Then there are some soothing herbs — aloe, slippery elm, and marshmallow root — that coat and calm an irritated surface, a bit like a soothing tea. DGL, a gentle form of licorice, settles inflammation in your stomach and upper gut. Zinc carnosine is a gut-specific zinc and the most-studied part of the blend for actually healing the lining. There is also N-acetyl glucosamine and citrus pectin to support the mucus layer over the cells, plus smaller amounts of chamomile, cat's claw, MSM, and quercetin for extra calming support. It is made by Designs for Health, a practitioner brand from Connecticut you mostly get through a clinician or their own store. The exact amounts are on your label, so check your tub.
